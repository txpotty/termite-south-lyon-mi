Liquid Termiticide Applications

Liquid termiticide applications are a common and highly effective method for creating a protective barrier around your home's foundation. This involves applying a liquid termiticide to the soil around the perimeter of your house. Termites foraging through the treated soil are exposed to the termiticide, which they then carry back to the colony, effectively eliminating the entire colony. This method provides a continuous barrier that prevents termites from entering your home. We pay close attention to details like trenching and rodding to ensure the termiticide is applied correctly and reaches the necessary depth for optimal protection.

Termite Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems offer an alternative or supplementary approach to liquid treatments. These systems utilize bait stations strategically placed around your property. The bait contains a slow-acting termiticide that termites consume and carry back to the colony, leading to the gradual elimination of the entire colony. Baiting systems are particularly useful in situations where liquid applications are challenging or for ongoing monitoring and prevention. They offer a less invasive option in some cases and provide long-term colony management. We choose the best method or combination of methods based on your specific situation and preferences.

Common Termite Problems and How We Solve Them

Termites can manifest in various ways, and recognizing the signs is crucial for early detection and intervention. Ignoring these indicators can lead to extensive and costly damage over time. At South Lyon Termite, we have years of experience dealing with all types of termite infestations and are equipped to handle any situation. We understand the common problems homeowners in Brighton and surrounding areas face when it comes to termites and have refined our techniques to provide effective and lasting solutions. Don't let these issues escalate; contact us at the first sign of trouble.

Mud Tubes on Foundation Walls

Mud tubes, also known as 'mud tunnels,' are one of the most common and visible signs of subterranean termites. These pencil-sized tubes are constructed by termites using soil and wood particles as protective pathways between their underground colonies and food sources (your home's wooden structures). Finding mud tubes on your foundation walls, crawl space, or even interior walls is a clear indication of termite activity. These tubes allow termites to travel safely from their moist underground environment to the drier wood above ground, protecting them from predators and dehydration. If you see mud tubes, it means termites are actively foraging for food within your home. South Lyon Termite will meticulously inspect the mud tubes to determine the direction of termite movement and pinpoint the location of the colony. We then apply targeted treatments to eliminate the termites using these pathways and disrupt their access to your home. Our goal is to not only remove the visible tubes but to eliminate the colony responsible for their construction, preventing further activity and damage. We will also advise you on steps you can take to make your foundation less attractive to termites, such as grading the soil away from the foundation and removing wood debris.

Damaged or Hollow-Sounding Wood

Termites eat wood from the inside out, so the exterior may appear perfectly normal while the interior is being severely damaged. Tapping on wood surfaces with a screwdriver or other solid object can reveal hollow-sounding areas, indicating termite activity within the wood. Damaged wood may also appear darkened, blistered, or otherwise compromised. As termites consume the cellulose in the wood, they create a network of tunnels and galleries, leaving behind only a thin veneer of the original material. This can significantly weaken structural elements like support beams, floor joists, and wall studs. If you suspect damaged or hollow-sounding wood, it's crucial to have it inspected by a professional to assess the extent of the damage and confirm the presence of termites. South Lyon Termite can accurately diagnose the cause of damaged wood and provide effective solutions. We identify the specific areas of infestation and implement targeted treatments to eliminate the termites within the wood. Depending on the severity of the damage, we can also advise on or provide recommendations for structural repairs needed to restore the integrity of your home. Early detection of damaged wood can prevent more extensive and costly repairs down the line. We utilize specialized tools to detect termite activity within wood that may not be visible to the naked eye.

Discarded Termite Wings

Swarming termites, which are reproductive termites that leave the colony to establish new ones, shed their wings after finding a mate and a suitable location for a new colony. Finding small piles of discarded, translucent wings near windowsills, doorways, or light sources is a strong indication of a recent termite swarm. Swarming typically occurs during certain times of the year, often after rain, as termites are attracted to light. While a swarm itself doesn't necessarily mean an active infestation within your home, it indicates that a mature colony is present nearby and potentially looking to establish a new colony on or in your property. These swarms are a natural part of the termite life cycle and are a sign of a well-established colony actively reproducing. South Lyon Termite can identify discarded wings and use this as evidence to initiate a thorough inspection of your property to locate the source of the swarm and any potential points of entry. We will then develop a targeted treatment plan to eliminate the existing colony and prevent future swarms from establishing new colonies on your property. Understanding the timing and location of swarms helps us focus our inspection efforts and deliver the most effective preventative measures. We can also help you identify potential nesting sites and conditions that may be attractive to swarming termites.

Subterranean Termite Swarms (Flying Termites)

Seeing flying termites, often called 'swarmers,' emerging from your home, especially during warmer months, is a definite sign of a nearby termite colony. Swarming termites are often mistaken for flying ants, but there are key differences in their appearance and behavior. Termite swarmers have straight antennae, a uniform waist, and two pairs of wings equal in size, while flying ants have bent antennae, a pinched waist, and forewings longer than their hindwings. Swarming is the method by which termites reproduce and spread, and it signifies that a mature colony has reached a significant size. While the swarmers themselves are not the ones causing damage to your home, their presence indicates that a colony is actively feeding and growing within or near your property. Their emergence from your home is a clear signal that a hidden infestation is likely present within the structure. South Lyon Termite can quickly identify if you are dealing with termite swarmers and initiate an immediate inspection to locate the source of the swarm and the main colony. We will then implement a targeted treatment plan to eliminate the colony and prevent further swarming within your home. We understand the urgency of dealing with a termite swarm and will respond promptly to your call. We can also offer advice on how to prevent future swarms from entering your home, such as sealing cracks and crevices around windows and doors.

Sagging Floors or Ceilings

Significant termite damage can compromise the structural integrity of your home, leading to noticeable sagging in floors or ceilings. This is a serious indicator of advanced termite infestation and requires immediate professional attention. When termites extensively damage the wooden support structures like floor joists, beams, and roof trusses, the building's weight can no longer be adequately supported, resulting in visible sagging, buckling, or uneven surfaces. This type of damage indicates a long-standing and large infestation that has significantly weakened the structural elements of your home. Addressing this level of damage often requires not only termite elimination but also potentially costly structural repairs. South Lyon Termite has the expertise to assess the extent of structural damage caused by termites and implement comprehensive treatment plans to eliminate the active infestation. While we focus on termite eradication, we can also provide recommendations for qualified contractors who can repair the structural damage. Our priority is to stop the termites from causing further damage and to help you restore the safety and stability of your home. We work in conjunction with structural engineers and contractors when necessary to ensure that your home is not only termite-free but also structurally sound. Ignoring sagging floors or ceilings can lead to dangerous structural failures, so prompt action is essential.

Bubbling or Peeling Paint

Termite activity can sometimes cause paint to bubble, peel, or blister on walls and other surfaces. This can occur when termites damage the wood beneath the paint, or when moisture associated with termite activity affects the paint's adhesion. As termites consume wood and create tunnels close to the surface, they can cause the paint to detach from the underlying material. Moisture associated with subterranean termite activity, as they bring moisture from the soil into their tunnels, can also lead to paint issues. While bubbling or peeling paint can have other causes, it's important to consider termites as a possibility, especially if it's occurring in areas with known wood framing or near the foundation. If you notice unexplained bubbling or peeling paint, it's wise to investigate further. South Lyon Termite can inspect the affected areas to determine if termites are the underlying cause. We will then implement targeted treatments to eliminate any active infestations and advise on necessary repairs to the damaged surfaces. Identifying peeling paint caused by termites allows for early intervention before more significant structural damage occurs. We can differentiate between paint issues caused by termites and those caused by other factors like water leaks or poor paint application.

Tight-Fitting Doors and Windows

Termite damage to door and window frames can cause them to become warped, swollen, and difficult to open or close. As termites consume the wood, they can weaken and distort the frames, leading to friction and sticking. The moisture associated with termite activity can also cause wood swelling, exacerbating the problem. If you notice that your doors or windows are suddenly becoming difficult to operate, and you rule out other potential causes like humidity or foundation settling, termite damage should be considered. This is particularly true if the difficulty is localized to specific doors or windows. South Lyon Termite can inspect the problematic doors and windows to determine if termite damage is the culprit. We will then implement effective treatments to eliminate any active infestations within the frames and surrounding walls. While our primary focus is on termite eradication, addressing the termite problem is the first step in resolving the issue with your doors and windows. Depending on the extent of the damage, repairs or replacement of the affected frames may be necessary. Early detection of termite activity in door and window frames can prevent the need for costly replacements. We can also advise on preventative measures to protect your doors and windows from future termite infestations.
